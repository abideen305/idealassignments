What is essay writing all about?

An essay is a dialogue. An imaginary conversation between worlds where the writer communicates with the body of knowledge he is working with, the readers and himself, is your most relevant research concern. The news and surprises that an author always has in store for us through an essay awaken that infinity that we all are. Thanks to the paper, we can recover in a dialogical way the living ideas of human beings through time and space. The essay consists of three fundamental parts: introduction, development, and conclusion. The introduction is usually short, but it all depends on our writing scope. Its function is to introduce the reader to the subject that we will deal with and, if necessary, make him aware of what has been said about the issue so far. In it, the hypothesis is presented. The hypothesis is that which we seek to carry out, clarify or sustain throughout our essay, and the body of the text will develop around it. It is, in itself, the seed from which the essay is born. It is the first thing to master to know how to do an essay. Development is the body of the essay. It covers most of the text and presents the arguments that clarify and support our hypothesis. Here, if the essay requires academic rigor, the critical apparatus (citations, notes, and references) necessary for our arguments to be solid will be inserted. The conclusion is the final part of the essay. We can recapitulate the main lines of argument following a line from the hypothesis and end by giving our point of view or final resolution of the issue.
